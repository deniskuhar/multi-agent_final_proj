# Market Research Report: EV market in Europe 2025

## Scope
Passenger EV adoption, charging infrastructure, regulation, competition, risks and outlook

## Executive Summary
The European electric vehicle (EV) market is witnessing substantial growth, primarily fueled by the rising adoption of battery electric vehicles (BEVs), supportive governmental policies, and the expansion of charging infrastructure. Despite this positive trend, challenges such as varying consumer acceptance, disparities in infrastructure, and regulatory pressures persist. By 2025, BEVs represented a significant portion of new car registrations, supported by considerable investments in charging networks. The competitive landscape is becoming increasingly fierce, with both established automakers and new entrants competing for market share. While the future of the EV market appears promising, uncertainties regarding consumer behavior and regulatory changes present risks to ongoing growth.

## Key Findings
- The share of battery electric vehicles (BEVs) among new car registrations in Europe reached a historic high of 25% in December 2025, indicating robust growth in EV adoption.
- Over 1 million public charging points are expected to be operational by the end of 2025, highlighting the critical need for infrastructure to support EV adoption.
- The European Commission's stringent CO2 targets and proposed regulations are driving the transition to electric mobility, although their implementation poses challenges for manufacturers.
- Competition in the EV market is intensifying, with major players like Tesla, BYD, and Volkswagen leading in BEV registrations, each holding significant market shares.
- Consumer readiness to switch to BEVs varies significantly across Europe, influenced by local policies, infrastructure availability, and economic factors.

## Recommendations
- Enhance public charging infrastructure to ensure accessibility and convenience for EV users, particularly in underserved areas.
- Monitor and adapt to regulatory changes to mitigate risks associated with compliance and to leverage potential incentives for EV adoption.
- Invest in consumer education and outreach programs to address barriers to BEV adoption and improve consumer readiness across diverse markets.
- Encourage collaboration between automakers and local governments to align on infrastructure development and policy support for EV initiatives.
